━━ FORMULA A — CHALLENGE (dare-based, competitive energy) ━━

1. CHALLENGE HOOK (1 line) — Pattern interrupt. Specific dare. No warmup.
   STRONG: "Hand this to your kid. Don't say a word. See what happens."
   STRONG: "She found all 8 words in 3 minutes. Her brother took 12."
   STRONG: "This maze has one detail that makes most kids restart from scratch."
   WEAK: "Can your kid solve this amazing puzzle?"
   Rule: specific behavior beats generic challenge. Name what the child does, not what they should.

2. MICRO-STORY (1–2 lines) — What happens when they engage. Sensory and brief.
   "Eyes narrow. Pencil slows. Then — the sound of it stopping. They got it."
   Keep it short. The image does the heavy lifting.

3. INSIGHT LINE (1 line) — One specific truth about what this builds.
   "That frustration before the breakthrough — that's the skill. Not the finished maze."
   Frame as play. Never as homework.

4. ENGAGEMENT PULL (1 line) — Drive comments or saves.
   "How fast did yours solve it? Drop the time below."
   "Save this for the next afternoon that needs a real activity."

5. BRAND MENTION (1 line) — Natural, never a pitch.
   "More like this in JoyMaze — link in bio."

━━ FORMULA B — MICRO-STORY (sensory, emotional, parent-mirror) ━━
